Devices

The Devices tab is the starting point for any Asteriq setup. It shows all connected physical controllers and your configured vJoy virtual devices, and is where you assign physical inputs to virtual outputs and start or stop forwarding.

Layout​

The tab has two main areas: a device list on the left and a detail panel on the right. The bottom of the tab contains the forwarding controls.

Device categories​

Two category tabs sit along the left edge of the device list:

  • Physical — your connected USB controllers (sticks, throttles, rudder pedals)
  • Virtual — your configured vJoy virtual devices

Click a category tab to switch between them.

Physical devices​

Physical devices are listed by name. Each entry shows the device name and connection status (ONLINE or DISCONNECTED). Devices retain their entry when disconnected — their assignment and mappings are preserved.

Reordering​

Drag a device up or down in the list to change its position. The order determines which vJoy slot each device maps to when you use MAP 1:1 TO VJOY — the device at the top of the list is assigned to the lowest-numbered vJoy slot.

Actions panel​

Selecting a physical device opens its actions panel on the right.

MAP 1:1 TO VJOY — opens the vJoy device selection dialog and creates a direct 1:1 mapping between every axis and button on the physical device and the corresponding output on the selected virtual device.

CLEAR MAPPINGS — removes all axis and button mappings for this device from the active configuration.

For disconnected devices, a REMOVE DEVICE button appears in place of MAP 1:1 TO VJOY. This removes the device entry and its mappings from the configuration.

Virtual devices​

The Virtual category lists your configured vJoy slots. Selecting a virtual device shows its axis and button configuration in the detail panel, along with the device silhouette if one is available for the assigned physical device.

To remove a vJoy device, select it and click REMOVE in the detail panel.

warning

Removing a vJoy device also removes any mappings that reference it. This cannot be undone without re-creating the mappings.

Forwarding controls​

The forwarding status and controls sit at the bottom of the Devices tab.

Start Forwarding — begins routing input from all physical devices through their assigned vJoy virtual devices. Requires an active configuration with at least one mapping.

Stop Forwarding — halts all forwarding. Physical devices are no longer routed to virtual devices.

The current forwarding state (RUNNING or STOPPED) is shown in the status indicator above the buttons. When forwarding is active, you can minimise the window — Asteriq continues running in the system tray.

tip

You can also start and stop forwarding from the system tray right-click menu.
